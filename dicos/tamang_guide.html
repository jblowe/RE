Alphabetical order

The alphabetical order follows the devanagari order.

Structure of entries

The Tamang word in bold is followed by an indication of part of speech in small capital letters explained in the abbreviations list. If the word is a borrowing this is indicated next. A translation in Nepali, French and English follows. If you tap on the entry, illustrative sentences may appear. This is indicated by an arrow. We regret that they are only translated in French in this version.

Transcription

Tamang words are transcribed in the International Phonetic Association system [IPA], except that c has been retained to mean IPA [ts] च, according to the common Nepali usage.

The raised number in front of a word is its tone, meaning the tune, melody and voice quality that the word is pronounced with. In the Risiangku pronunciation, [¹] indicates a high falling melody, [²]a high level melody , [³] a low rising melody and [⁴] a low falling melody. Additionally, tones 3 and 4 have a whispery voice quality. Tamang tones are delicate nuances of pronunciation, much less differentiated from each other than Chinese tones for instance; nevertheless they serve to distinguish the meaning of words, for instance ³mar means ‘butter’ but ⁴mar means ‘gold’; also ¹sang-pa means ‘to sweep’ but ²sang-pa means ‘to dry’. Zero indicates an uncertain tone. We are conscious that there are mistakes in the transcription of tones and the compiler would be grateful to receive corrections for a new version of the dictionary.

Nepali words, either as translations, or as words borrowed into Tamang, appear in italics. They are transcribed in the standard way. To avoid confusion, note that in Tamang words, according to the IPA system, [j] designates the sound what is written y य in Nepali orthography; but in Nepali words j transliterates ज and sounds about like IPA [dz].
